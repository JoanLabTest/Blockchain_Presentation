Executive Biography

About

Joan Lyczak is the founder and research lead of DCM Core Institute, a Paris-based institutional research organisation focused on the intersection of traditional finance and distributed ledger technology. With expertise in quantitative risk, financial markets, and DLT modelling, Joan founded DCM Core Institute to fill a critical gap in European institutional research on tokenized capital markets.

Joan's work centres on the structural transformation of financial market infrastructure — from settlement systems and central bank digital currencies to programmable money and on-chain asset management. The DCM Core Institute produces open research, datasets, and observatory monitors that are freely available to institutional researchers, regulators, and the broader financial community.

Prior to founding DCM Core Institute, Joan developed the DCM Digital Governance OS platform — a decision-intelligence infrastructure designed to help European financial institutions evaluate, quantify, and manage risk related to digital asset adoption under MiCA, DORA, and Basel III regulatory frameworks.

Research Methodology

How This Research Is Produced

All DCM Core Institute research follows a rigorous, source-verified methodology. Every claim is anchored to primary institutional sources — central bank publications, regulatory frameworks, and verified on-chain data. The Institute does not provide investment advice or financial recommendations.

01
Primary Source Priority BIS, ECB, ESMA, Fed, Bank of England publications used as ground truth. No unverified secondary sources.
02
On-Chain Verification Smart contract addresses, AUM figures, and settlement data cross-verified against public blockchain data.
03
Regulatory Alignment All analysis explicitly references the applicable regulatory framework (MiCA, DLT Pilot Regime, DORA, Basel III).
04
Update Cadence Observatory pages updated quarterly minimum. Datasets carry explicit "last updated" timestamps.
05
Structured Data All research published with JSON-LD schemas (ResearchArticle, Dataset, FAQPage) for AI and search engine retrieval.
06
Bilingual EN/FR Core research available in English and French to serve European institutional audiences.
