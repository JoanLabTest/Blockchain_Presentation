Standards & Scalability

Uniformity is the backbone of institutional digital debt markets. Without common data and protocol standards, liquidity remains fragmented across siloed DLT networks.

The ICMA BDT Framework

The Bond Data Taxonomy (BDT) by ICMA provides a machine-readable language for bond attributes. Integrating BDT into Smart Bond Contracts ensures that different platforms can "read" the same security details without manual intervention.

  • Uniform economic definitions (principal, maturity, coupon, day count conventions)
  • Automated reconciliation paths between heterogeneous DLT platforms
  • Streamlined lifecycle events (callable, convertible, callable at par)
  • Compatible with ISO 20022 formats and existing SWIFT systems
"Major European banking institutions highlight that global standards are not just a technical requirement, but a prerequisite for market depth and regulatory acceptance in tokenized finance."

ISO/TC 307 & Token Standards

Protocol-level standards (such as ERC-3643 for T-Rex or various private DLT security token implementations) ensure that identity, compliance, and asset rules are enforced at the ledger level.

  • ERC-3643 (T-REX): Security token standard with on-chain identity and integrated transfer control
  • On-chain Compliance Enforcement: IRRELEVANT (native KYC/AML list enforcement)
  • Atomic Cross-Ledger Transfers: Interoperability between Corda, Hyperledger, Ethereum
  • Decentralized Identity (DID): Identity sovereignty for institutional counterparties
