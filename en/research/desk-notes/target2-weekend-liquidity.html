TARGET2 Weekend Settlement Frictions: Empirical Telemetry

Observation: Telemetry captured during weekend clearing cycles reveals a operational gap between continuous, 24/7 tokenized asset settlement channels and traditional TARGET2 liquidity accounts. The bottleneck occurs at the retail sweep interface during high-volume periods.

SCT Inst / RT1 Telemetry: Peak latency reached 412ms on Saturday at 14:00 CET, compared to a baseline average of 15ms on weekdays. TARGET2 liquidity exposure gap measured at 0.14 bps.

Analysis: Direct funding via TARGET2 remains closed over the weekend. Automated sweep algorithms designed for Tier-1 LCR buffering faced operational dry-spells of up to 45 minutes before secondary clearing routes activated. This suggests that wholesale DvP settlement utilizing off-ledger cash legs introduces an asynchronous queuing risk that cannot be resolved solely via DLT performance upgrades.
