Chapter 01

Executive Summary

As of Q1 2026, the transition of legacy financial message-based systems toward atomic, ledger-native infrastructures has reached a critical tipping point. The DCM Core Institute estimates the total market capitalization of tokenized real-world assets (RWA) at $24.8 Billion, representing an 18% year-over-year increase.

Key Strategic Findings

  • Standardization Gap: 70% of issuers now cite the lack of identification standards (e.g., TFIN ID) as the primary barrier to secondary market liquidity.
  • Institutional Shift: Pilot programs have evolved into production-grade Distributed Financial Market Infrastructures (D-FMI).
  • Fixed Income Dominance: Tokenized bonds remain the primary driver, accounting for 62% of the aggregate market value.

Chapter 04

Institutional Adoption Trends

Major Tier-1 institutions like Société Générale (FORGE), HSBC (Orion), and UBS have successfully integrated ledger-native securities into their distribution networks. The focus has shifted from "technical capability" to "commercial scale" and "regulatory alignment" under the MiCA framework.

Inter-bank settlement layers, such as the Regulated Liability Network (RLN), are now becoming the backbone for multi-asset institutional settlement.

Chapter 05

The Infrastructure Layer

Institutional preference is clearly concentrating toward EVM-compatible environments (Ethereum L2s) and high-performance public ledgers like Stellar and Polygon. Security, finality time, and cross-ledger interoperability remain the dominant technical selection criteria.

Chapter 06

Market Standards & Structure

The DCM Core Institute has pioneered two critical frameworks for market scalability:

  • TFIC (Technical Financial Instrument Classification): A granular taxonomy for multi-ledger assets.
  • TFIN ID: The first universal identification standard providing a single source of truth across siloed registries.

Chapter 08

Future Outlook (2026-2030)

By 2030, we anticipate that 10% of global GDP will be stored or traded on ledger technologies. Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) and tokenized deposits will provide the programmable cash leg necessary to unlock the full potential of global tokenized capital markets.
