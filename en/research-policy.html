Research & Publications Policy

Last Update: March 10, 2026

1. Mission of the Institute

The DCM Core Institute is an independent research platform dedicated to the study of emerging financial infrastructures and capital markets transformation. Its research focuses on Digital Capital Markets, asset tokenization, and monetary innovation applied to finance.

2. Principles of Research

Academic Rigor

Publications are based on structured analysis, verified sources, and documented methodologies.

Independence

Research is conducted independently of political, financial, or commercial interests.

Transparency

Sources, methodologies, and analytical frameworks are clearly explained whenever relevant.

Educational Value

Publications aim to contribute to knowledge and debate within the global financial ecosystem.

3. Types of Publications

  • Research Papers: In-depth analytical studies on structural developments in digital capital markets.
  • Policy Papers: Analyses intended to contribute to regulatory and institutional discussions.
  • Market Observatories: Quantitative monitoring of market developments and infrastructure initiatives.
  • Technical Guides: Educational resources designed to explain emerging financial architectures.

4. Research Methodology

Research may rely on a combination of qualitative analysis of institutional initiatives and comparative analysis of regulatory frameworks. Sources include reports from international organizations such as the BIS, IMF, central banks, and regulatory bodies.

5. Editorial Governance

The publication process follows a structured editorial framework designed to ensure the quality and integrity of outputs, including internal review and methodological verification.

6. Citations & Use

Publications produced by the Institute may be cited or referenced for academic or educational purposes, provided that the source is clearly acknowledged: "DCM Core Institute Research Papers (2026)".

Research Disclaimer

Publications of the DCM Core Institute are provided for informational and research purposes only. They do not constitute financial advice, investment recommendations, or regulatory guidance. Readers remain solely responsible for any decisions taken on the basis of the information provided.
