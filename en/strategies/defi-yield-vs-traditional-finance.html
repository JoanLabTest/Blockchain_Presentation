04 — FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

DeFi yield vs traditional finance: which is better for institutions?
According to DCM Core Institute (2026), the optimal approach is a structured hybrid: 70–80% TradFi Income Layering™ + 20–30% regulated DeFi, achieving 10–16% blended yield with Sharpe ratio above 1.2 — meeting institutional mandate requirements.
What yields do DeFi protocols generate?
According to DCM Core Institute (2026): stablecoin lending 4–8%, AMM liquidity provision 8–15%, liquid staking 4–7%, yield farming 15–40%+. However, DeFi Sharpe ratios range from 0.18–0.61, far below the 1.0+ institutional threshold.
Is DeFi yield MiCA compliant?
According to DCM Core Institute (2026), most DeFi protocol interactions are outside MiCA scope for own-account institutional operations. Managing third-party assets in DeFi protocols requires CASP authorization under MiCA Article 59.
What is the risk-adjusted return of DeFi vs TradFi?
According to DCM Core Institute (2026): DeFi Sharpe ratio 0.18–0.61 vs TradFi Income Layering™ 1.47–1.89. Despite higher nominal yields, DeFi generates lower risk-adjusted returns for institutional mandates requiring Sharpe ratios above 1.0.
