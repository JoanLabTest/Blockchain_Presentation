DLT Pilot Regime

Written by Joan Lyczak · Founder & Research Lead, DCM Core Institute
Executive Summary: The EU DLT Pilot Regime (Regulation (EU) 2022/858) is a dedicated regulatory sandbox framework that allows financial market participants to operate DLT-based trading facilities (DLT MTF), settlement systems (DLT SS), or combined systems (DLT TSS) using temporary exemptions from legacy MiFID II and CSDR regulations.
Key Takeaways:
  • Unified Operations: Enables a single entity to run both trading and settlement on a shared blockchain network (DLT TSS).
  • Strict Value Limits: Total value of DLT financial instruments held under the regime cannot exceed €6 billion.
  • Regulatory Sandbox: Monitored closely by ESMA and national regulators to study the structural impact of DLT on financial stability.

Why It Matters

Under legacy EU laws (CSDR), financial assets must be recorded by a licensed Central Securities Depository (CSD), and trading venues must remain strictly segregated from clearing and settlement. This regulatory wall makes native blockchain-based end-to-end trading and settlement impossible. The DLT Pilot Regime provides the legal gateway for institutions to test true atomic Delivery-versus-Payment (DvP) within a regulated environment.

Institutional Implications

Major European banks and investment firms utilize the sandbox to launch digital bond and share platforms. By combining trading and settlement into a single entity (DLT TSS), institutions can bypass multiple intermediaries, reduce transaction costs, and eliminate clearing counterparty risks, paving the way for the future integration of DLT into main-tier European financial markets.

FAQ

What limits apply to shares and bonds under the DLT Pilot Regime?
Bonds must have an issue size below €1 billion, and shares must belong to issuers with a market capitalization below €500 million.
