Canton Network
Confidential Enterprise Blockchain

Decentralized and interoperable DLT infrastructure, enabling institutions to execute complex smart contracts while ensuring absolute data privacy.

Bank-oriented platform, featuring native privacy via UTxO (Causal) and seamless interoperability through Daml smart contracts.

Privacy Model Institutional Pricing

The Canton Promise

Solving the institutional trilemma: offering the universal reconciliation of a public blockchain with the privacy of a private database.

Native Privacy
Unlike Ethereum, transaction data on Canton is only shared between explicitly designated stakeholders in the Daml smart contract.
Global Interoperability
The Global Synchronizer allows private bank networks (Subnets) to transact atomically with each other without risky bridging.
Daml Smart Contracts
A language designed specifically for finance, ensuring deterministic execution and naturally modeling legal rights and obligations.

Execution Model & Privacy Guarantees

A rigorous approach to data segmentation for financial markets.

Visibility Segmentation
Only nodes participating in a contract see its state. Other nodes, including global validators, only see cryptographic hashes.
Deterministic Finality
The Canton model mathematically guarantees immediate finality without possibility of forks, essential for high-value asset settlement.
Notary Model
A decentralized notary service ensures ordering and prevents double-spending without having access to transaction content.

Governance & Networks

Each institution manages its own participant node, ensuring full sovereignty over data and local compliance.

  • Participant Node Operator
    Hosted by the bank, it holds the keys and exclusively manages contracts (Daml) where the institution is a stakeholder. Total data isolation.
  • Global Synchronizer (Notary)
    Decentralized sequencer responsible for global consensus and encrypted routing. **Zero-Knowledge** model: it cannot see contract content.
  • Governance & Participation Model
    Decentralized network operated by a consortium of market infrastructures (e.g., Goldman Sachs, Microsoft, Deloitte). No single central authority.

In Summary: Canton for CIOs

Canton offers the **composability** of public networks with the **privacy** of private databases. It is the ideal infrastructure for interoperability between sovereign banking sub-networks.
