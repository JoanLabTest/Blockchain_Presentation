Research Governance Framework

The formal charter detailing analytical guardrails, methodological limitations, independence standards, and data coverage boundaries for DCM Core intelligence assets.

Section 01 / Core Charter

Editorial Doctrine & Independence Statement

The DCM Core Institute operates as an autonomous, non-commercial analytical repository dedicated to the structural mapping of tokenized capital markets and digital financial infrastructure.

Conflict & Independence Declaration: DCM Core does not accept sponsorships, promotional funding, or commercial retains from token issuers, DLT protocols, or financial institutions. All methodologies, scoring matrices, and systemic alerts are executed with absolute quantitative objectivity. Our research aims strictly to advance macroprudential understanding, remaining distinct from retail sentiment, investment solicitation, or commercial market-making.

Section 02 / Operational Limits

Research Scope & Systemic Exclusions

Our research boundaries are engineered to prioritize systemic, highly institutional assets over broader speculative cryptomarkets.

  • Covered Assets: Tokenized Sovereign Debt (T-Bills), Regulated Stablecoins / Electronic Money Tokens (EMTs), Tokenized Commercial Cash (T-Cash), and Tier-1 Custodied Real-World Assets (RWAs).
  • Systemic Exclusions: Unregulated offshore algorithmic wrappers, speculative utility tokens lacking institutional bank custodians, and highly illiquid fractionalized non-financial assets.
  • Private Ledger Bounds: While private permissioned bank ledgers represent a substantial share of atomic settlement volume, their opaque reporting nature restricts real-time auditing. DCM Core relies on officially declared quarterly bank disclosures and validated pilot summaries for these nodes.

Section 05 / Risk Safeguards

Analytical Boundaries

Users must interpret DCM Core deliverables strictly under the following academic and analytical constraints:

  • Signal ≠ Prediction: Weekly Signals represent structural vulnerabilities and transmission channels based on backward-looking and real-time data. They do not constitute forward price forecasts or sentiment projections.
  • Score ≠ Rating: Risk scores assigned via GDARI or MICA frameworks are comparative infrastructural risk metrics, not credit rating assessments under regulatory rating agency definitions (CRA).
  • Map ≠ Exhaustive Exposure: Topology maps illustrate key institutional relays and do not represent total systemic counterparty exposure.
