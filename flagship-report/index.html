Core Thematic Focus Areas

Macro-Financial Linkages

Quantitative modeling of contagion channels between traditional banking infrastructure and digital asset liquidity pools. Assessing stablecoin runs and tri-party repo dependencies.

Liquidity Stress in DLT

Evaluation of automated market makers (AMMs) under severe market shocks. Analysis of impermanent loss vectors, oracle manipulation risks, and fragmented liquidity across L2 rollups.

Regulatory Arbitrage

Comparative legal matrix of the EU's MiCA framework versus the US fragmented enforcement-led approach (SEC/CFTC) and Asian retail-friendly regimes (SFC/MAS).

Institutional Protocol Resilience

Security audits and economic stress tests of major Smart Financial Infrastructure protocols. Assessing the viability of DORA-compliant smart contracting environments.

Executive Summary Key Takeaways

  • Systemic De-risking: Institutional adoption requires cryptographic proof of reserves coupled with real-time liquidity monitoring to prevent cascading liquidations.
  • The MiCA Premium: EU-domiciled Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) are trading at a regulatory premium, attracting significant wholesale institutional capital.
  • Stablecoin Hegemony: USD-backed stablecoins now process settlement volumes rivaling major credit card networks, necessitating swift prudential oversight.
  • RWA Tokenization: The true value of DLT lies not in native crypto-assets, but in the efficiency gains of tokenizing Real-World Assets (RWAs) for wholesale settlement.
