DORA Blockchain Risk Framework: Resilience for DLT Infrastructures

The **Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA)** represents a fundamental shift in how the European financial sector must view its technology. For institutions deploying **Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT)**, DORA is the ultimate test of operational reliability.

CRO Perspective: DORA isn't just about preventing hacks; it's about proving that your institutional registry can survive a vendor failure, a node desynchronization, or a regional cloud outage.

1. ICT Risk Management for Blochain Nodes

DORA Article 5 mandates robust ICT risk management frameworks. In a blockchain context, this extends to:

  • Node Redundancy: Strategic placement of nodes across geographies and providers (multi-cloud strategy).
  • Consensus Integrity: Monitoring for ledger forks or notary desynchronization that could impact settlement finality.
  • Bridge Security: Rigorous ICT testing for the interoperability layers between traditional ledgers and DLTs.

2. Incident Reporting and Notarization

Under DORA, financial entities must classify and report major ICT-related incidents to competent authorities. DCM Core's **Audit Trail Architecture** ensures that every infrastructure event is cryptographically notarized, providing the "Single Source of Truth" required for regulatory reporting.

3. Digital Operational Resilience Testing

Institutions must conduct annual stress tests of their ICT systems. For DLT, this means simulating "Network Halt" scenarios. How does your liquidity model react if the SWIAT notary is unavailable for 6 hours? How does Canton handle a subnet failure? DCM Core's **Simulation Engine** builds these DORA-required scenarios into the daily dashboard.
