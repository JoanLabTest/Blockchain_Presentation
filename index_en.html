Module 0: Demystifying "Crypto"

Translating Tech into Banking Language

1. What is Blockchain?

It is nothing more than a Ledger.

Analogy:

Today, every bank has its own Excel file. To make a transfer, we must reconcile my Excel with yours.

The Disruption:

With Blockchain, we all share the same Excel file (Google Sheet) in real-time. No more reconciliation needed.

2. What is a Wallet?

It is your Bank Account + Your Signature.

Public Address = IBAN:

You can give it to anyone to receive funds.

Private Key = Electronic Signature:

It is the unique secret code to validate a transfer. If you lose it, you lose access (hence the importance of Custodians like Fireblocks).

Confusion to absolutely avoid:

Do not confuse Cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ether) which are volatile speculative assets, with Digital Assets (Security Tokens) which are infrastructures to exchange banking assets (Bonds, Cash) more efficiently.

Crypto-monnaies Bitcoin, Ethereum = Speculative
Digital Assets Bonds, Cash = Infrastructure
